# Bang Bang Chicken Power Bowl

Original recipe: https://www.instagram.com/p/DZdrscmpYeq/

Servings: 4
Prep time:
Cook time: 
Total time: 

## Indredients:
- 1 lbs chicken thighs, finely diced
- 1/2 tsp avocado oil
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 large lime, halved
- 1 tbsp flour (or cornstarch)
- High protein rice (or other rice)
- 1/4 head cabbage, thinkly shredded
- 1 carrot, thinly shredded
- 3-4 Persian cucumbers, thinly sliced
- 3 scallions, finely sliced
- 1 jalapeño, thinly sliced (may remove seeds)
- handful of cilantro, roughly chopped
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 3 tbsp rice vinegar
- 2 tbsp tamari (or soy sauce)
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- 3 tbsp mayo
- 2 tbsp siracha
- 1.5 tbsp unsweetend ketchup
- 1/2 stp powdered monkfruit
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
- salt
- pepper


## Instructions:
### Chicken:
1. Finely dice 1lb chicken thighs into small pieces.
2. Add chicken to a bowl and drizzle with 1/2 tsp avocado oil, 1 tbsp soy sauce.
3. Season with salt, pepper, 1 tbsp garlic powder, 1 tbsp onion powder, 1 tbsp smoked paprika, the juice of half a large lime, 1/2 tbsp powdered monkfruit or sweetener, & optional: 1 tbsp gluten free flour or cornstarch.
4. Mix well, then pop in the oven at 400F for 32 mins, or airfry at 400F for 22 mins.

### Base:
1. For the base, I used my Kaizen high protein lowcarb rice, but use any rice you like. Cook to instructions on package.

### Cucumber Cabbage Slaw:
1. Thinly shred 1/4 head cabbage with a mandolin, add to a bowl with 3-4 thinly sliced Persian cucumbers, 1 thinly shredded carrot, 3 finely sliced scallions, 1 thinly sliced jalapeño (remove seeds if you like), and a large handful of roughly chopped cilantro.
2. Add 1 tbsp sesame oil, 3 tbsp rice vinegar, 2 tbsp tamari or soy sauce, a pinch of salt and pepper.
3. Mix really well, taste and adjust.
4. Optional: add 1 tbsp of toasted sesame seeds for a nice crunch.

### Creamy Bang Bang Sauce:
1. Mix together 3 tbsp mayo, 2 tbsp sriracha, 1.5 tbsp unsweetened ketchup, the juice of half a lime, 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper, and 1/2 tsp powdered monkfrit.
2. Mix well, taste, and add 2 tbsp of water to thin out the sauce if you wish.

### Serving:
- When you’re ready to eat, just assemble! I divided everything into 4 servings: I plated up 1 serving Kaizen lowcarb rice as the base, 4oz cooked chicken, a cup of slaw, drizzled some of the sauce, topped with fresh cilantro & sesame seeds.
