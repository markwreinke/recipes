# Rossini’s Marinara

Original recipe: https://www.reddit.com/r/Old_Recipes/comments/gaog3h/comment/fp135yv/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=web3x&utm_name=web3xcss&utm_term=1&utm_content=share_button

## Indredients:
- 3 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1/2 medium onion, chopped
- 1 28 oz. can of crushed tomatoes
- 1 tsp. oregano
- 1/4 tsp. parsley, chopped
- Salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions:
1.  Brown the garlic lightly and sauté the onions together in olive oil for 5-10 minutes.
2.  Add tomatoes, salt, pepper, oregano, and parsley.
3.  Cook slowly, simmering and uncovered about 30-45 minutes.
4.  Add water if it becomes too thick.
5.  Serve hot over cooked pasta, such as linguini or spaghetti. Recipe can be doubled or tripled and frozen. Cook longer for larger batches.

## Notes:
- Because tomato sauces made with canned tomatoes tend to be acidic, try adding a 1/4 tsp or more of sugar.
- Here’s a good primer on the matter of sugar in marinara. https://www.epicurious.com/ingredients/sugar-tomato-sauce-secret-article
- Many people add between 2-8 tbsp. of extra virgin olive oil depending on the size of the recipe. Some also add a bit of red pepper flakes.
